** Lesson 13: Importing Your Knowledge Base

Prerequisites: [[requires:lesson:observability][Lesson 12: Observability]]

If you have an org-roam directory (or any directory of ~.org~ files with ~:ID:~ properties), you can register it as a federated KB instance in MAE.

*** Prerequisites
- Your org files need ~:PROPERTIES: :ID: <uuid> :END:~ drawers.
- Nested subdirectories are supported (recursive import).
- Files without ~:ID:~ are skipped (counted in the report).

*** Step 1: Register
#+begin_example
:kb-register MyNotes ~/RoamNotes
#+end_example
Or ask the AI: "import my KB at ~/RoamNotes"

MAE will recursively walk the directory, parse all ~.org~ files, and report results:
#+begin_example
Registered 'MyNotes': 2,342 nodes, 4,891 links
  Health: 45 orphans, 12 broken links, 3 duplicate IDs
#+end_example

*** Step 2: Verify
- ~:kb-instances~ — shows your registered instance with node count.
- ~:kb-health~ — detailed health report (orphans, broken links, namespaces).
- ~SPC n f~ or ~SPC h s~ — search should find your notes.

*** Step 3: Use
Your notes are now searchable from ~:help~, ~SPC h~, and the AI agent. The AI can find and summarize your notes just like built-in help.

*** Step 4: Update after edits
When you edit org files externally, refresh the graph:
#+begin_example
:kb-reimport MyNotes
#+end_example

*** Data safety
- MAE *never writes* to your org directory (except ~eor-instance.org~ sentinel).
- Your org files are the source of truth. The index is always re-derivable.
- Back up your org files with git. The SQLite cache is disposable.

*** Unregister
#+begin_example
:kb-unregister MyNotes
#+end_example
Removes from registry and frees memory. Your org files are untouched.
